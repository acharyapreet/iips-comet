About the Conference

üìú Background

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence, particularly with the rise of large language models, is fundamentally transforming the global workforce. As AI automates routine tasks, there are growing concerns about job displacement, the need for new skills, and the ethical implications of these powerful technologies. The traditional view of AI as a tool for pure automation is giving way to a more nuanced understanding of its role as a partner in human work. This shift necessitates a new approach that puts people at the center of technological change.

üéØ Rationale

The rationale for focusing on human-centric AI is to ensure that the future of work is not just efficient, but also equitable, meaningful, and sustainable. By prioritizing human values and needs in the design and deployment of AI, we can mitigate potential risks like algorithmic bias, data privacy violations, and job dissatisfaction. This approach aims to foster a positive human-AI relationship, one that empowers individuals, enhances creativity, and drives innovation by leveraging the unique strengths of both humans and machines.

Objectives

  • Explore how human-centric AI can transform business strategies and models.
  • Share ideas on aligning AI adoption with ethics, inclusivity, and sustainability.
  • Discuss workforce transformation, reskilling, and human-AI collaboration.
  • Build bridges between academia, industry, and policymakers for responsible AI.
  • Provide recommendations for AI governance in business contexts.

Key Themes

  • Human-Centric AI: Principles and Practices
  • AI Ethics, Trust, and Accountability
  • The Future of Work and Workforce Transformation
  • AI-Driven Business Models and Innovation
  • AI for Sustainability and Inclusive Growth
  • Policy, Regulation, and Governance

Tracks and Subthemes

This track focuses on how AI is reshaping organizational strategy, workforce management, and ethical governance in business. It invites scholars and practitioners to reflect on management practices that integrate AI responsibly while keeping humans at the center.

Subthemes:

  • AI in Strategic Decision-Making and Leadership: Leveraging AI insights for competitive advantage; Data-driven decision-making and strategic agility; Leadership styles in AI-augmented organizations.
  • AI and the Future of Work: Workforce reskilling, upskilling, and lifelong learning; Redesign of work roles: human‚ÄìAI collaboration; Organizational culture and employee well-being in the AI era.
  • Ethics, Governance, and Policy in Management: Ethical frameworks for AI adoption; Trust, transparency, and accountability; Corporate governance and compliance in AI-driven ecosystems.

This track emphasizes how AI is opening up opportunities for entrepreneurs, startups, and innovators. It examines how AI can drive business model innovation, foster inclusive growth, and address societal challenges through entrepreneurship.

Subthemes:

  • AI-Powered Startups and Entrepreneurial Ecosystems: Opportunities and challenges for entrepreneurs; Startup case studies and AI-led disruption; Scaling AI ventures and navigating investment landscapes.
  • Business Models for the AI Economy: Platform-based and data-driven business models; Customer experience personalization through AI; Monetization of AI tools, products, and services.
  • Inclusive and Sustainable Entrepreneurship: Social entrepreneurship using AI; AI-driven solutions for sustainability and SDGs; Democratizing access to AI for underserved communities.

This track highlights the technical foundations and innovations in AI that enable ethical, transparent, and sustainable adoption in business. It encourages contributions from researchers, computer scientists, and engineers working on the core technology side of AI.

Subthemes:

  • AI Models, Algorithms, and Explainability: Advances in machine learning, deep learning, and generative AI; Explainable AI (XAI) for transparency; Bias detection, fairness, and responsible algorithm design.
  • Human-Centered Systems and Interfaces: Human‚ÄìAI interaction and user-centered design; NLP, conversational AI, and multilingual applications; AI for human augmentation.
  • AI Applications for Sustainable and Secure Futures: AI for smart cities, climate change, and green technologies; Cybersecurity, privacy-preserving AI; Building trustworthy, scalable, and secure AI infrastructure.

About Our Institution

About Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya

Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), also known as Devi Ahilya University, is a prominent public university located in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India. Established in 1964, the university was initially known as the University of Indore and later renamed in honor of the legendary queen of the Maratha Empire, Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar, known for her contributions to society, art, and architecture.

DAVV offers a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, diploma, and doctoral programs across disciplines like science, arts, commerce, law, management, engineering, education, and social sciences. The university has numerous academic departments and affiliated colleges that cater to different fields of study, including technology, health sciences, and professional courses.

About International Institute of Professional Studies

The International Institute of Professional Studies (IIPS) is a renowned academic institution under Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), Indore. Established in 1992, IIPS is a pioneer institute to provide a new dimension to professional education. IIPS is a realm where education meets innovation, and dreams take flight.

The Institute has been providing exceptional opportunities to students for the past 32 years. IIPS is known for its integrated and professional courses in the fields of management, computer science, and commerce. The institute has gained a reputation as a center of excellence, particularly for offering five-year integrated programs, which are designed to provide students with comprehensive education and professional training.
